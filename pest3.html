CHARCOL ROT

Symptoms:

This disease causes lesions on the coleoptile, leaf blade, leaf sheath and glumes, being most noticeable on the leaf blade and glumes. Initially it appears as minute brown dots, later it starts becoming cylindrical or oval to circular in shape. Several spots coalesce resulting in drying up of entire leaf.


causes :

Charcol rot is a fungal disease that infects the coleoptile, leaves, leaf sheath, panicle branches, glumes, and spikelets. Its most observable damage is the numerous big spots on the leaves which can kill the whole leaf. When infection occurs in the seed, unfilled grains or spotted or discolored seeds are formed.

pesticides :

Seed treatment with Pseudomonas fluorescens 10g/kg of seed followed by seedling dip of 2.5 kg or products/ha dissolved in 100 litres and dipping for 30 minutes.
